The Rust Room · Restorations

Left
To Die.
Brought
Back.

We find them in the wet basements of Osaka, under tarps in Kyushu warehouses, behind the ramen counters in Shibuya. Machines left to die — Neo Geo cabs with peeled decals, shuttered Sega Super Nova stations, Taito cocktail tables warped by decades of heat.

We bring them home. We strip, reweld, repaint, rewire. We hunt replacement CRTs at flea markets from Frankfurt to Fukuoka. And we ship them back out, breathing — to collectors in Seoul, to bar owners in Brooklyn, to kids in Kreuzberg who have never touched a 1990 coin slot.

OUR /
CARTRIDGE /
ARCHIVE.

Six thousand game cartridges under one roof. N64, Super Nintendo, Mega Drive, PC Engine, Neo Geo AES — plus shelves of rare PAL-B MSX tapes and a locked drawer of Famicom Disk System blanks nobody in Berlin has seen in thirty years.

Every cart authenticated by the shop, boxed, catalogued and archived under a JSON-backed index we keep backed up on a PowerBook G3 because we like the sound the drive makes. Come pick through the shelves. Bring something you lost.
