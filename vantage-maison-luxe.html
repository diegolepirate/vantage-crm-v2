03 · Process

How a Maison Luxe project unfolds

1

Listen

We start with a six-hour conversation. No samples, no measurements — just who you are, what you've loved, what you've outgrown.

2

Visit

We walk the space at the three worst hours of its day. Bad light reveals more than good furniture ever will.

3

Plan

A 3D walkthrough, a material wardrobe, and a single, stubborn design proposal. No options — we make a decision and defend it.

4

Build

Our artisan network — all within two hours of Paris — executes. We visit the site twice a week and hate shortcuts.

5

Live

We hand over the keys, then come back at six months and two years. A home is a conversation we plan to continue.

01

Light

Every room begins as a choreography of sunrise, noon, and dusk. Before we think about lamps, we study how natural light enters the space across an entire year. We visit at dawn, at golden hour, on overcast afternoons. Only then do we layer artificial light — always in service of what nature already offered, never in competition with it. A well-lit room is one where you forget the switch exists.

↓ See more

Case study — The Belleville flat

A north-facing 70 m² apartment that its owner described as "a basement with a view." We spent two weeks mapping shadow paths, then removed a 3-metre section of interior wall to let the weakest southern light travel deeper into the plan.

We installed full-spectrum LED cove lighting tuned to shift from 2700K at dawn to 4000K at midday and back to 2400K at dusk — a circadian rhythm inside the architecture itself.

The owner reports that for the first time in a decade, she wakes up without an alarm.

02

Space

Emptiness is expensive and underrated. We protect volume the way a musician protects silence — as the structural element that gives everything else meaning. Most homes are over-furnished by 40%. Ours are designed to leave air between the objects, between the rooms, between the person and the wall. Space is not absence; it is the active ingredient.

↓ See more

Case study — Île Saint-Louis duplex

A 220 m² duplex where we removed more than we added. Two bedrooms became one generous suite. A study became a reading alcove with a single chair and a single lamp.

The client's brief mentioned "storage for 1,200 books." We negotiated it down to 600 — and then built a library so quiet that visitors lower their voices without being asked.

We believe in the rule of the unnecessary chair: every room should contain one piece of furniture that exists purely to be looked at, never sat in.

03

Material

Oak that darkens, linen that crumples, travertine that fingerprints, brass that patinates. We love surfaces that keep a record. A house finished in the wrong materials starts dying the day it is handed over; a house finished in the right ones only begins to live at that moment. We refuse engineered veneers, MDF millwork, and any finish that cannot be repaired by a human hand in twenty years.

↓ See more

The Maison Luxe material wardrobe

We maintain a working library of 340 material samples, all sourced from European artisans within a two-hour radius of Paris. Each sample is aged, wet, scratched, and photographed under six lighting conditions before it enters the wardrobe.

Our rule: no material enters a project that we have not personally seen at ten years old. We drive to old houses, old hotels, old shops. We touch walls that have been touched by thirty years of hands.

Beautiful at month one is easy. Beautiful at year thirty is the entire brief.

04

Memory

Your grandmother's mirror, the chair from the old flat, the rug that no longer matches anything — we build rooms that honour what you already love. A Maison Luxe project begins with an inventory of objects you refuse to throw away. Those objects are not obstacles to the design; they are the design. Everything else we add is in negotiation with them.

↓ See more

The rule of the inherited object

On the first visit, we ask clients to show us three objects they would rescue from a fire. Those three objects become the anchors of the project — colour, scale, texture, and mood all emerge from them.

A Wassily chair from a divorced grandfather. A lamp from a first apartment. A rug that survived three moves. These are not decoration; they are the client's autobiography in physical form.

We do not redesign your life. We make a room your life can continue inside of.

×
05

Silence

The best interior is the one you stop noticing — because it finally lets you notice yourself. We design for acoustic quiet, visual quiet, and the deepest quiet of all: the absence of decisions the room forces you to make. A great room asks nothing of you. It simply holds you.

↓ See more

The acoustic brief

Every Maison Luxe project now includes a dedicated acoustic consultant. We specify wool carpets over felt underlay, bookshelves as diffusers, curtains as absorbers, and limit hard parallel surfaces that cause standing waves.

The target reverb time in a living room is 0.4 seconds — slightly longer than a recording studio, slightly shorter than a café. You cannot hear the number, but you feel it the moment you walk in.

A silent room is not a dead room. It is a room that lets its inhabitants speak.
