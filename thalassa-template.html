Our Philosophy

Rooted in tradition.
Liberated by curiosity.

Every dish at Thalassa begins with a conversation — with the fisherman who knows the tides, the farmer who tends olive groves older than memory, the forager who reads the hillside like a map. We honor these voices through a cuisine that respects the raw ingredient above all.

Chef Nikos Papadakis brings a philosophy shaped by Cretan heritage, Parisian technique, and the Japanese principle of ichigo ichie — one encounter, one chance. Each plate is that chance.

The Setting

A table above
the Aegean.

Perched on the caldera's edge, our terraces offer an unbroken view of the sea at every table. As the sun sets, the space transforms into a theater of light, stone, and sky.

The Chef

Nikos
Papadakis

Born in Crete, trained at École Ferrandi in Paris and refined through a transformative year in Tokyo's Kaiseki tradition. Nikos returned to Greece with a singular vision: to honor the purity of Greek ingredients through the lens of global technique.
