01 — The Vision

A photograph is a held breath.

I don't chase the decisive moment. I wait for it — through blue hour, through silence, through the third cup of coffee on a slow Aegean morning. The best frames arrive when everyone has stopped performing.

My work sits between editorial precision and the intimacy of film. I shoot digitally and on medium-format Portra 400, I light small, and I trust the room. Texture, grain, and the honest weight of skin matter more than any trend.

Clients come to me for a single thing: pictures that still feel like them a decade later.

— Portfolio reel

Three countries. Twelve months. One lens.

02 — Process

Five steps from idea to archive.

i.

Consultation

A call, a coffee, a moodboard. We define the feeling before the frame.

ii.

Direction

Location scouting, references, wardrobe notes, a shot list kept deliberately loose.

iii.

Shoot

Medium-format film + Leica digital. Small crew, real light, generous pace.

iv.

Edit

A hand-graded selection. No AI retouch — skin is skin, grain is grain.

v.

Delivery

High-res archive, print-ready masters, and an optional cotton-rag book.

05 — FAQ

Questions, answered.

If yours isn't here, write to studio@marlowegrey.com.

What does a session cost? +

Portrait sessions start at €1,800 for a half-day including direction, shoot, edit and a 20-image digital archive. Editorial and campaign budgets are quoted against the usage and crew required.

What's the turnaround? +

A hand-graded gallery lands in your inbox within 14 days. Rush delivery in 72 hours is available on request.

Who owns the rights? +

Copyright stays with the studio. You receive a clear, written licence for the agreed usage — personal, press, commercial or editorial — with reasonable renewals.

Do you travel? +

Yes — roughly a third of the year. Destination shoots are quoted flat with travel, a small film budget and a local assistant already included.

How do you retouch? +

Honestly. I grade tone and contrast, clean stray distractions, and leave the rest. No AI, no skin-smoothing, no liquify. If a line belongs to you, it belongs in the frame.
